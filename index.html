About Me

My passion has always been organization, whether that is organizing my day in my planner, organizing the spices in my kitchen pantry, or organizing the parts of a project. As a perfectionist, I value quality over quantity and ensure that everything I do is up to my best standards. I’m always willing to take on new and challenging tasks because there’s always room to learn and grow. The “jack of all trades” is what I aim to be.

I am detailed oriented and a self-motivated learner who is passionate about discovering the unique intersection between business, technology and design. I love to explore and redefine the boundaries between everyday life and technology, believing that the right digital experience can truly transform one’s life.

I graduated from Cornell University, choosing to study User Experience, Digital Culture and Productions, and Business because I believe research and understanding the background is just as important as the design and development of technology itself.

Currently, I am the Project Manager for a creative agency that manages an expansive portfolio of digital projects across various industries with budgets ranging from $10K-$5MM+. We cover a range of digital initiatives: digital marketing strategy, website design and development, content marketing, email, displays, and social media.

In my career, I’m driven by curiosity, to ask questions but also to provide immediate and long-term solutions. The idea is to not just keep clients satisfied now but ask the questions “How can I go above and beyond what he/she asked? How can I be of better service to my clients?” As a Project Manager, I lead by example, understanding what needs clients have and providing possible solutions, but also thinking what needs clients might have down the road. With every project, I try to translate the overall goal into manageable parts that both my team and the client can digest. I won't stop until my clients are satisfied with the results.

I live in NYC and in my free time, you can either find me cooking up new recipes that bring me back to my childhood or hitting a new PR at the gym.
